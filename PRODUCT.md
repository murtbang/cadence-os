# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Cadence is for one primary user: the owner using a Samsung Galaxy Tab A7 Lite in landscape as the source-of-truth device. The app is used as a personal LifeOS dashboard for quick, repeated glances throughout the day, especially to see upcoming meetings, todo items, habit progress, notifications, and daily goal state without digging.

The primary screen should stay simple and glanceable. Complexity belongs in side menus, deeper views, and focused task surfaces where the user has intentionally chosen to inspect or change something.

## Product Purpose

Cadence exists to keep the user's day visible without turning personal planning into a project-management system. It combines habits, todos, calendar visibility, focus sessions, daily goal tracking, notifications, Discord inputs, and weekly review into one tablet-first dashboard.

Success means the Galaxy Tab can sit in landscape and answer the user's next practical question quickly: what is coming up, what needs attention, what habits remain, and how today's focus is tracking.

## Brand Personality

Minimal, bold, intuitive.

Cadence should feel compact, direct, and quietly confident. It should make high-value daily signals easy to read at a glance, while still allowing deeper workflows when the user opens a specific view.

## Anti-references

Do not make Cadence feel gamified, too corporate, or cluttered. Avoid generic SaaS dashboard tropes, productivity-bro styling, decorative complexity, and any visual direction that makes the dashboard feel more like a command center than a calm personal surface.

The previously attempted dark Pentagon Pizza Index / DEFCON-style direction was rejected and should not be revived without an explicit new request.

## Design Principles

Tablet truth first: the Samsung Galaxy Tab A7 Lite in landscape is the real canvas. Laptop behavior can remain acceptable, but the tablet viewport decides layout, density, and interaction choices.

Glance before management: the dashboard should answer daily questions quickly. Editing, configuration, review, and dense detail can live behind side navigation or focused views.

Simple surface, deeper side paths: keep the home dashboard clean and legible, but allow complexity in menus and secondary screens when it supports real workflows.

Mobile-native interaction: prefer Ionic React components and mobile-friendly controls where they improve touch ergonomics, platform behavior, and maintainability.

Bold through clarity: use visual weight, hierarchy, and spacing to make the dashboard decisive without adding clutter, gamification, or corporate polish.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The Galaxy Tab A7 Lite landscape experience is the primary accessibility target. Text must be readable at a glance, touch targets should be near 48px where practical, and controls should remain forgiving for repeated touch use.

Motion should be limited and purposeful, with reduced motion respected by default. Status and priority should not rely on color alone; shape, labels, iconography, position, or text should reinforce meaning where needed.
